about

Natalie received her Masters in clinical psychology from Azusa Pacific University. She is also a Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA). Her professional experience includes the assessment and treatment of children, adolescents, and their families with a variety of developmental disabilities and behavioral disorders in school, home, community and clinical settings. Natalie provides services to families and children, as well as diagnostic evaluations in Spanish. She also has experience working in a psychiatric hospital setting, conducting psychological batteries, risk assessments, and individual consultations, as well as facilitating group counseling sessions on an intensive child inpatient unit. As an active member in the Autism community, Natalie has presented on relevant topics impacting the population at the Tools for Transformation Conference, and has provided training to counselors for the Special Needs Network’s Joe Patton Academy Camp (Camp JPAC). In addition, she is a member of the American Psychological Association and Behavior Analyst Certification Board.

Natalie has received specialized training in a variety of therapeutic modalities, including Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavior Therapy, Play therapy, and Pivotal Response Training. She enjoys utilizing a variety of techniques and methodologies to tailor her approach to each unique client. Her current research interests include competency to stand trial evaluations, specifically with ethnic minority juveniles, in the criminal system.

mission

To provide quality services and support to children, families, and the community by:

  • Gaining an understanding of each child and their family, including culture and values.
  • Tailoring services for children and families to be individualized and flexible.
  • Developing programs with the individual family’s goals and desires in mind to work as a team.
  • Providing a sense community and resources to support families.
  • Focusing on strengths while improving weaknesses.
  • Providing accessible service and applying effective strategies and methodologies.
  • Staying current with a variety of practices including psychotherapy and psycho-education.
  • Maintaining a stance of compassion, acceptance, and respect.
